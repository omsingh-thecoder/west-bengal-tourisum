Gangasagar

A sacred destination where the Ganges meets the sea, offering spiritual and cultural experiences.

About Gangasagar

Gangasagar, located in West Bengal, is a prominent pilgrimage site where the Ganges River meets the Bay of Bengal. It is revered for its sacredness and the annual Gangasagar Mela, attracting thousands of devotees and tourists who come to take a holy dip in the river and offer prayers at the Sagar Island temple.

Key Attractions

Gangasagar Temple

A historic and sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, attracting pilgrims from all over India.

Meeting of Rivers

Witness the confluence of the Ganges River and the Bay of Bengal, a spiritually significant sight.

Gangasagar Mela

The annual fair held in January, featuring various cultural and religious events, and attracting pilgrims and tourists.

Plan Your Visit

  • Take a holy dip at the confluence of the Ganges and the Bay of Bengal
  • Visit the Gangasagar Temple for spiritual reflection
  • Experience the vibrant atmosphere of the Gangasagar Mela (if visiting in January)
  • Explore local markets and sample traditional Bengali cuisine
  • Enjoy a boat ride to get a unique view of the river meeting the sea

Timings and Entry Fee

The key attractions in Gangasagar are generally open throughout the day, but specific timings may vary.

  • Gangasagar Temple: Open daily from early morning to late evening
  • Gangasagar Mela: Annual event in January, timings may vary
  • Entry Fee: Free for the Gangasagar Temple and riverfront areas

Note: Specific events and boat services may have varying hours and fees. Please check local listings for details.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Gangasagar is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities. Here's a seasonal breakdown:

  • Winter (November to February): Mild temperatures and ideal conditions for pilgrimage and exploration
  • Summer (March to June): Hot and humid, less ideal for travel
  • Monsoon (July to October): Heavy rains may impact travel plans and accessibility
