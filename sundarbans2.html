About Sundarbans

The Sundarbans is a vast mangrove forest in the coastal region of the Bay of Bengal. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the famous Royal Bengal Tiger. This unique ecosystem is known for its rich biodiversity and is the largest mangrove forest in the world, spanning across India and Bangladesh.

Things to Do

  • Take a guided boat safari through the mangrove channels
  • Visit the Sundarbans National Park
  • Birdwatching at Sudhanyakhali Watch Tower
  • Explore the Bhagabatpur Crocodile Project
  • Experience local village life and culture
  • Visit the Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary
  • Enjoy the sunset at Jambudwip

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Sundarbans is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and dry. This period offers the best conditions for wildlife spotting and boat safaris. Here's what you can expect in different seasons:

  • Winter (October to February): Ideal weather, best time for wildlife spotting
  • Summer (March to May): Hot and humid, but still good for bird watching
  • Monsoon (June to September): Heavy rainfall, many areas become inaccessible
