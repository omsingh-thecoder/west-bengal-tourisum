Dooars

A picturesque region in North East India known for its lush greenery, wildlife, and tea gardens.

About Dooars

The Dooars is a region in the foothills of the eastern Himalayas, stretching across North Bengal and Assam. Known for its sprawling tea gardens, dense forests, and abundant wildlife, it is a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The region offers stunning views of the Himalayas and rich biodiversity, making it an ideal location for eco-tourism and relaxation.

Key Attractions

Tea Gardens

Explore the scenic tea gardens of Dooars, which offer a serene environment and beautiful landscapes.

Forest Reserves

Visit the lush forest reserves like Jorpokhri Wildlife Sanctuary and Buxa Tiger Reserve to experience the rich flora and fauna.

River Views

Enjoy the tranquil views of rivers like the Teesta and Rangeet, which offer a refreshing and scenic experience.

Local Culture

Immerse yourself in the local culture by visiting traditional villages and experiencing the lifestyle of the indigenous communities.

Plan Your Visit

  • Explore the lush tea gardens and enjoy a peaceful walk through the plantations
  • Visit the wildlife sanctuaries and take part in wildlife safaris
  • Experience the local culture and traditional festivals
  • Relax by the rivers and enjoy the natural beauty of the region
  • Take a guided tour to explore the diverse flora and fauna of the region

Timings and Entry Fee

Timings and entry fees may vary for different attractions in Dooars.

  • Tea Gardens: Typically open from early morning to late afternoon
  • Wildlife Sanctuaries: Open daily, timings may vary; entry fees apply
  • Local Markets and Cultural Spots: Generally open throughout the day

Note: It is advisable to check with local authorities or tour operators for specific timings and entry fees for each attraction.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Dooars is during the cooler months from October to March. Here’s a seasonal breakdown:

  • Winter (October to March): Pleasant weather, ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities
  • Summer (April to June): Hot and humid, less preferable for travel
  • Monsoon (July to September): Heavy rainfall may affect travel plans and accessibility
