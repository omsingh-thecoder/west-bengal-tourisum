Howrah Bridge

An iconic symbol of Kolkata and a marvel of engineering

About Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge, officially known as Rabindra Setu, is a balanced cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River in West Bengal, India. Commissioned in 1943, it's one of the longest cantilever bridges of its type in the world and is often considered to be among the most famous landmarks of Kolkata and West Bengal. The bridge connects the cities of Kolkata and Howrah, spanning the Hooghly River.

Key Attractions

Night View

Experience the breathtaking view of the illuminated bridge at night, reflecting on the Hooghly River.

Nearby Markets

Explore the bustling markets near the bridge, including the famous Mallick Ghat Flower Market.

River Cruises

Take a river cruise on the Hooghly to get a unique perspective of the bridge and the city skyline.

Plan Your Visit

  • Walk across the bridge for panoramic views of the Hooghly River and Kolkata skyline
  • Visit during sunrise or sunset for stunning photography opportunities
  • Explore the nearby Howrah Station, one of the busiest railway stations in India
  • Take a boat ride under the bridge for a unique perspective
  • Visit the nearby Mullick Ghat Flower Market for a colorful experience

Interesting Facts

  • The bridge is 705 meters (2,313 ft) long and 71 feet wide
  • It carries a daily traffic of approximately 100,000 vehicles and countless pedestrians
  • The bridge uses 26,500 tons of steel, with the floor of the bridge consisting of 14,500 tons of high-tensile alloy steel
  • It's the sixth-longest bridge of its type in the world
  • The bridge was built without nuts and bolts, using rivets instead

Best Time to Visit

Howrah Bridge can be visited year-round, but the experience can vary with the seasons:

  • Winter (November to February): Pleasant weather, ideal for walking across the bridge
  • Summer (March to June): Hot and humid, but the bridge is less crowded
  • Monsoon (July to October): Rainy season offers a unique atmosphere, but be cautious of slippery surfaces

For the best views, visit during sunrise or sunset. The bridge is particularly stunning when illuminated at night.
