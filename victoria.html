Victoria Memorial

A majestic monument of British Raj in the heart of Kolkata

About Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Kolkata, West Bengal, India, which was built between 1906 and 1921. It is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria (1819–1901) and is now a museum and tourist destination under the auspices of the Ministry of Culture. The memorial lies on the Maidan (grounds) by the bank of the Hooghly River, near Jawaharlal Nehru Road (formerly known as Chowringhee Road).

Key Attractions

Architectural Marvel

Admire the stunning Indo-Saracenic revivalist style of architecture, blending British and Mughal elements.

Museum Collection

Explore a vast collection of artifacts, paintings, and sculptures from the colonial era.

Memorial Gardens

Stroll through the beautifully landscaped gardens surrounding the memorial.

Plan Your Visit

  • Explore the museum's extensive collection of historical artifacts
  • Attend the Sound and Light show in the evening
  • Take a leisurely walk in the surrounding gardens
  • Visit the nearby St. Paul's Cathedral
  • Enjoy boating in the Hooghly River with a view of the memorial

Timings and Entry Fee

The Victoria Memorial is open to visitors from Tuesday to Sunday. The museum remains closed on Mondays and national holidays.

  • Museum Timings: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM
  • Garden Timings: 5:30 AM to 6:15 PM
  • Entry Fee for Indians: ₹30 for adults, ₹10 for children
  • Entry Fee for Foreigners: ₹500
  • Camera Fee: ₹50 (still camera), ₹250 (video camera)

Note: Timings and fees are subject to change. Please check the official website for the most up-to-date information.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Victoria Memorial is from October to March when the weather in Kolkata is pleasant. Here's what you can expect in different seasons:

  • Winter (November to February): Cool and comfortable, ideal for outdoor exploration
  • Spring (March to April): Mild weather, good for visiting before the summer heat
  • Summer (May to June): Hot and humid, but less crowded
  • Monsoon (July to September): Rainy season, the gardens are lush but outdoor activities may be limited
