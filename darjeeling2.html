Darjeeling

The Queen of Hills - Where Tea and Mountains Meet

About Darjeeling

Darjeeling, known as the "Queen of Hills," is a picturesque hill station in West Bengal, India. Famous for its tea plantations, stunning views of the Himalayas, and the UNESCO World Heritage Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, this charming town offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, colonial history, and vibrant local culture.

Key Attractions

Tea Gardens

Explore the lush tea gardens and learn about the art of tea production at Happy Valley Tea Estate or Glenburn Tea Estate.

Tiger Hill

Witness a breathtaking sunrise over the Kanchenjunga peak, the third-highest mountain in the world.

Toy Train Ride

Experience the UNESCO World Heritage Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, also known as the "Toy Train," for a scenic journey through the hills.

Plan Your Visit

  • Take a tour of world-famous Darjeeling tea estates and enjoy tea tasting sessions
  • Visit the Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park, home to red pandas and snow leopards
  • Explore the vibrant local markets like Chowrasta and Batasia Loop
  • Trek to nearby viewpoints for panoramic views of the Himalayas
  • Visit the Japanese Peace Pagoda for tranquility and stunning views
  • Experience the local Tibetan and Nepali culture at monasteries and cultural centers

Best Time to Visit

Darjeeling can be visited year-round, but each season offers a unique experience:

  • Spring (March to May): Pleasant weather, ideal for outdoor activities and viewing rhododendron blooms
  • Summer (June to August): Escape the heat of the plains, but be prepared for occasional rain
  • Autumn (September to November): Clear skies, perfect for mountain views and outdoor exploration
  • Winter (December to February): Cold weather with occasional snowfall, offering a different charm

The peak tourist seasons are from April to June and September to November. For the best views of the Kanchenjunga, plan your visit during October and November.

Local Cuisine

Darjeeling offers a unique blend of Tibetan, Nepali, and Bengali cuisines. Don't miss trying:

  • Momos (steamed or fried dumplings)
  • Thukpa (Tibetan noodle soup)
  • Sel Roti (sweet bread ring)
  • Darjeeling tea, of course!
  • Alu Dom (spicy potato curry)
  • Naga cuisine at Keventer's
