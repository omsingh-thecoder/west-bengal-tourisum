Murshidabad

A historical gem of West Bengal, steeped in royal heritage

About Murshidabad

Murshidabad, located in West Bengal, is a city rich in history and culture. Once the capital of Bengal under Mughal rule, it is home to several magnificent historical sites, including palaces, mosques, and gardens. The city's heritage reflects a blend of Mughal and British colonial influences.

Key Attractions

Hazarduari Palace

Explore the grandeur of the Hazarduari Palace, known for its 1000 doors and opulent architecture.

Katra Masjid

Visit the Katra Masjid, an impressive Mughal-era mosque with intricate designs and a serene ambiance.

Jahankosha Canon

Discover the Jahankosha Canon, one of the largest cannons in India, set amidst historical ruins.

Plan Your Visit

  • Tour the Hazarduari Palace and its museum
  • Admire the Mughal architecture of Katra Masjid
  • Stroll through the historical gardens and ruins
  • Visit the Murshidabad Archaeological Museum
  • Explore the local markets and taste traditional Bengali cuisine

Timings and Entry Fee

Murshidabad's key attractions are generally open throughout the week, but specific timings and fees may vary.

  • Hazarduari Palace: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM (Closed on Fridays)
  • Katra Masjid: Open from sunrise to sunset
  • Entry Fee for Hazarduari Palace: ₹50 for Indians, ₹100 for foreigners
  • Entry Fee for Katra Masjid: Free

Note: Timings and fees are subject to change. Please check the respective attraction's official website for the most up-to-date information.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Murshidabad is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing. Here's a seasonal breakdown:

  • Winter (November to February): Cool and comfortable, perfect for exploring historical sites
  • Spring (March to April): Mild weather, great for sightseeing before the summer heat
  • Summer (May to June): Hot and humid, but the city is less crowded
  • Monsoon (July to September): Rainy season, lush greenery but potential travel disruptions
